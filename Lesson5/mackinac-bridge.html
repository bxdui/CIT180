Facts about the Mackinac Bridge

The bridge cost $99.8 million at the time of construction, translating to $708 million in 2019. The total width of the roadway is 54 feet, with the outside lane 12 feet wide and the inside lane 11 feet wide. The median is 2 feet wide. When driving across the bridge, you will reach a max height of 200 feet above the water in the center of the bridge. The water below you is 250 feet deep. In comparison to the road, the support towers stand 552 feet above the water level and sink 210 feet below the water. The catwalk, curb, and rail width is 3 feet total per side. The bridge celebrated its 150 millionth vehicle crossing on September 6, 2009, and crosses a speculated average of 11,608 vehicles per day. The speed limit is 45 mph for regular vehicles, and 20mph for heavy trucks. Trucks are also mandated to keep a 500-foot following distance.
